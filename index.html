Preface

Introduction

Hello and welcome to Metroidvania Ability Compendium - a community-driven resource for anyone who:

  • Wants to create their own Metroidvania game and seeks inspiration;
  • Wants to explore their favorite games from a different angle;
  • Wants to explore new game design venues;
  • Has nothing better to do with their time than to read interesting things;

If you'd like to help with this project check out How to contribute. If you'd like to express appreciation give us a shout on Twitter or Facebook and share it with your friends.

Please be warned that this document contains spoilers. Links between abilities and specific games are hidden behind buttons which unroll the necessary information.

What is Metroidvania

Metroidvania games generally feature a large interconnected world map the player can explore, though access to parts of the world is often limited by doors or other portals that can only be opened after the player has acquired special items, tools, weapons or abilities within the game. Acquiring such improvements can also aid the player in defeating more difficult enemies and locating shortcuts and secret areas, and often includes retracing one's steps across the map.

Source Wikipedia

Your typical Metroidvania game is typically portrayed as a single large area or a set of large areas, broken up into many different rooms, corridors, and open spaces, with Respawning Enemies in most areas. Progress in the game is driven by the discovery of Video Game Tools (actions, abilities, inventory items) that allow the player to navigate obstacles and "unlock" new areas, while also serving as more than just a "key"; for example, a weapon powerful enough to destroy certain walls will often deal more damage to enemies, and the ability to climb walls could be used to avoid enemies as well as reach high places. The player will often pass many insurmountable obstacles as they explore the game, which they must backtrack to after finding the appropriate item/ability, often made easier by opening Doors To Before.

Source: TV Tropes

How to contribute

Adding content

  1. If you know how to use git and know how to read and modify YAML:

    • Make a fork of the repository;
    • Clone the repository;
    • Modify the YML files;
    • (Optional, requires grunt-cli) run npm install followed by grunt and check your changes in bin/html/index.html;
    • Add your details to credits.yml at the bottom;
    • Make a commit with a meaningful message (don't modify changelog.yml yourself);
    • Post a Pull Request;

  2. If you have a Github account:

    • Ensure you are logged in;
    • Go to the Issues tab in the repository;
    • Post a new issue detailing what you'd like added;

Providing template

If you're a capable designer who'd like to donate their time and abilities to support this project please either use Issues tab to notify me or send me an email directly.

                                                                  Analysis:

                                                                  Being the first or one of the first games that we can categorize as metroidvanias Metroid is responsible for a lot of things we take for granted today - open exploration of the world, collecting power-ups to enable new routes, backtracking or hunting for hidden power-ups. The strong core of the game has been subsequently reused and improved upon both in further installments and in other games which renders the original devoid of any especially intriguing characteristics, but not fully.

                                                                  All the technological limitations of the era and the platform create something to explore, though from the point of view of contemporary game design it's of little interest as most of the decisions made around those limitations is not very interesting to explore nowadays.

                                                                  But there is one very interesting concept in this game that could make a comeback in a modern game, even if in Metroid it served very little purpose - limiting the beam to a single power-up. A careful routing could create an interesting puzzle for the player to solve, though it's very important to avoid excessive amounts of backtracking in such a case. Skipping item aquisition fanfare would also be a wise choice.

                                                                  Description:

                                                                  A side-scroller platformer shooter.

                                                                  Analysis:

                                                                  For many Metroid II is the black horse of the family, despite in almost every aspect being superior to its predecessor. Many parallels could be drawn to even more infamous Zelda II: The Adventure of Link, though it wasn't as drastic of a change. However Metroid II is a very interesting piece to study when placed in the context of the other 2D metroid titles.

                                                                  The game features almost complete departure from traditional "bosses" found in majority of games. Instead the player fights the same minibosses multiple times in different environments which is a two-edged sword, more intensely boring the player but at the same time allows them to experience how the context shapes the seemingly identical encounters. It could've been additionally improved by allowing a combat with multiple metroids of the same of different types at the same time but most likely due to the technical limitations this wasn't done - such a move would've certainly increased the fun of the game.

                                                                  There is also the problem known from the first game, an abundance of identical passages and chambers. This only gets worse by the fact that the locations do not exactly belong in a flat or sensible space - plotting the map onto paper results in rooms overlapping each other or being longer/shorter/bigger/smaller depending on which route you take. Both of those things are huge issues.

                                                                  Description:

                                                                  A side-scroller platformer shooter.

                                                                  Analysis:

                                                                  Metroid: Zero Mission is a complete reimagination of the original metroid - it's technically a remake but it improves on every single aspect of the original. It's a very polished game and in terms of game design there are a few areas which are have to be explored further:

                                                                  1. The whole stealth section which is an elaborate mini-game yet at the same time is almost perfectly within the boundaries set by the main game - the player executes the same gameplay routines (traversing dangerous terrain and shooting at enemies) but the changed context and slight mechanics alterations make it into a very different game. There are only really two big accusations that can be made: the first enemy you encounter can't be fathomably avoided without raining an alarm; the sequence is a bit too long and repetetive.

                                                                  2. By looking at the sequence breaks in the game it is very clear the game was consciously designed with them in mind (as opposed to Super Metroid which has a comparable amount of sequence breaks but it's arguable whether they were intended or not). Whether that's better than unintentional sequence breaks is a matter of discussion, but the less hardcore players will certainly appreciate the fact that they can't get (semi-)stuck by accidentally doing things out of order.

                                                                  Energy Health Starts at 99HP, damage is dealt by touching enemies and their projectiles and can be restored by drops left from killing the enemies. Losing all Energy results in death.
                                                                  Jump Jump A regular jump, available from the beginning of the game. Has two versions - regular jump and spin jump. The latter is executed when Samus jumps while running and allows for greater speed in the air. Firing the weapon while performing a spin jump ends it.
                                                                  Beam Weapon Can be used to destroy enemies and blocks which unlock new passages.
                                                                  Morph Ball Morph Ball

                                                                  Makes Samus morph into a ball, effectively halving Samus's height, disabling Jump and Weapon. Allows passing through narrow passages. Allows jumping when Hi-Jump is collected.
                                                                  Long Beam Weapon Range Increases the range of the beam, useful for opening up pathways for Morph Ball.
                                                                  Charge beam Charge Weapon Allows charging the beam to shoot stronger bullets. Some enemies can only be damaged by charged beam.
                                                                  Energy Tank Health Expansion Increases the maximum HP by 100, a total of 12 can be collected in various hidden places. On hard mode each tank increases the maximum HP by 50.
                                                                  Missiles Strong Weapon Can be used to open red gates and deal more damage to enemies. They have limited ammo that is dropped by enemies and the ammo limit can be expanded.
                                                                  Missile Tank Ammo Expansion Increases the missile limit.
                                                                  Bombs Bombs

                                                                  Can be placed in Morph Ball form and used to destroy specific types of blocks and to jump.
                                                                  Plasma Beam Damage Upgrade

                                                                  Increases damage and makes the beam go through multiple enemies. When first collected it's marked as Unknown Item 1 and serves additionally as Key. The ability is activate and usable only in the final stretch of the game.
                                                                  Power Grip Ledge Hanging

                                                                  Allows grabbing the ledges, climb them up, jump to the side or down and fire when hanging. Can climb up into 1-tile tall passages by immeddiately morphing into Morph Ball.
                                                                  Ice Beam Enemy Freezer Allows freezing enemies and using them as platforms.
                                                                  Space Jump Infinite Jump

                                                                  Allows jumping infinitely up but only when performing the spin jump. When first collect it's marked as Unknown Item 2 and serves additionally as Key. The ability is activate and usable only in the final stretch of the game.
                                                                  Speed Booster Running
                                                                  • Allows Samus to reach a super speed after a short running start, during which she's invincible and damages/kills enemies.
                                                                  • Allows destroying special blocks on touch.

                                                                  When Samus crouches during a Speed Booster run she then enters a state during which a shinespark can be executed - an infinite directional dash which destroys the same blocks as Speed Booster.

                                                                  Super Missiles Strong Weapon Even stronger than regular missiles, can destroy their own specific type of block and open green doors.
