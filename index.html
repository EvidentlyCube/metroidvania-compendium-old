Preface

Introduction

Hello and welcome to Metroidvania Ability Library - a community-driven resource for anyone who:

  • Wants to create their own Metroidvania game and seeks inspiration;
  • Wants to explore their favorite games from a different angle;
  • Wants to explore new game design venues;
  • Has nothing better to do with their time than to read interesting things;

Please be warned that this document contains spoilers without any spoiler warnings. In the future you will be protected by the fact that specific examples of abilities in games will be by default hidden, but this is not present as of yet.

What is Metroidvania

Metroidvania games generally feature a large interconnected world map the player can explore, though access to parts of the world is often limited by doors or other portals that can only be opened after the player has acquired special items, tools, weapons or abilities within the game. Acquiring such improvements can also aid the player in defeating more difficult enemies and locating shortcuts and secret areas, and often includes retracing one's steps across the map.

Source Wikipedia

Your typical Metroidvania game is typically portrayed as a single large area or a set of large areas, broken up into many different rooms, corridors, and open spaces, with Respawning Enemies in most areas. Progress in the game is driven by the discovery of Video Game Tools (actions, abilities, inventory items) that allow the player to navigate obstacles and "unlock" new areas, while also serving as more than just a "key"; for example, a weapon powerful enough to destroy certain walls will often deal more damage to enemies, and the ability to climb walls could be used to avoid enemies as well as reach high places. The player will often pass many insurmountable obstacles as they explore the game, which they must backtrack to after finding the appropriate item/ability, often made easier by opening Doors To Before.

Source: TV Tropes

How to contribute

Adding content

  1. If you know how to use git and know how to read and modify YAML:

    • Make a fork of the repository;
    • Clone the repository;
    • Modify the YML files;
    • (Optional, requires grunt-cli) run npm install followed by grunt and check your changes in bin/html/index.html;
    • Add your details to credits.yml at the bottom;
    • Make a commit with a meaningful message (don't modify changelog.yml yourself);
    • Post a Pull Request;

  2. If you have a Github account:

    • Ensure you are logged in;
    • Go to the Issues tab in the repository;
    • Post a new issue detailing what you'd like added;

Providing template

If you're a capable designer who'd like to donate their time and abilities to support this project please either use Issues tab to notify me or send me an email directly.

List of Abilities

Routing

These abilities are about unlocking new routes: pathways and passages to new areas and shortcuts to old areas.

Jumping

Jump

The regular ability to jump. In almost any platformer game it's an intrinsic ability of the protagonist.

May be used in top-down games as a time-to-time or regular mechanic for added gameplay.May be used in non-action games (like RPGs or 3/4 perspective games).May be an ability that needs to be collected by the protagonist.
  • Metroid A regular jump, available from the beginning of the game. Has two versions - regular jump and spin jump. The latter is executed when the protagonist jumps while running and allows for greater speed in the air. Firing the weapon while performing a spin jump ends it.
Increased Jump Height

Increases the height of the primary Jump.

May also increase the height of all other types of jumps (eg. Wall Jump, Secondary Jumps).
    Secondary Jumps

    Allows the protagonist to perform a second jump in mid-air.

    May allow more jumps as part of a single power-up or other powerups.May include a collectible which regenerates the secondary jump in mid-air.
      Infinite Jump

      Variation of Secondary Jumps, gives the protagonist the ability to jump in mid-air indefinitely.

      May be paused/stopped when protagonist is damaged, making it possible to restart only after landing.May only allow moving vertically up, being stopped after moving horizontally and then requiring landing to restart.May only allow moving in a single diagonal direction to limit its usability.
        Wall Jump

        Allows jumping off the wall in mid-air.

        May disallow climbing a single column by pushing the protagonist far away from the wall on jump that they get back to the wall a little lower than where they jumped from.May only allow jumping off specific types of walls/disallow jumping off specific types of walls.May allow climbing a vertical column indefinitely.May be limited to a single wall-jump between landings.May completely disallow returning back to the wall that was jumped off by forcing inertial movement until landing (makes it really hard to aim).

          Accessiblity

          These abilities are about making the game more accessible:

          Weapons

          Weapon Range

          Increases primary/secondary weapons range which makes it easier to defeat enemies.

          May allow destroying blocks and enemies which are further away from the player using Weapon.
          • Metroid Increases the range of the beam. The power-up is completely optional.

          Player Properties

          Health

          Health is a protagonist property that can be reduced by touching enemies, projectiles, environment or the background. Usually when reduced to zero the protagonist dies and a save has to loaded.

          Game might not have any health and the player will be a One-Hit-Point Wonder.May be restored by drops from the enemies.May be restored by saving the game.May restore itself in the background.May restore itself in specific plot-points in the game.May be non-restorable.May be expanded by collecting (optional) power-ups.
          • Metroid Starts at 99HP, damage is dealt by touching enemies and their projectiles and can be restored by drops left from killing the enemies. Losing all Energy results in death.
          Health expansion

          A power-up which increases the max health of the protagonist.

          Might be in the form of a collectible power-up.Might be awarded at specific plot-points.
          • Metroid Increases the maximum HP by 100, a total of 6 can be collected in various hidden places.
